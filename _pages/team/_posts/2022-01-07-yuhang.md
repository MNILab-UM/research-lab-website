Yuhang Li is a forth-year Ph.D. student in the lab. Prior to joining the Li Lab, he received his Bachelorâ€™s degree from East China Normal University. He is interested in investigating the neural mechanisms that underlie the social impairment present in autism spectrum disorder (ASD), by utilizing a range of methodologies, such as EEG, fNIRS, fMRI, and hyperscanning. His Ph.D. project has three primary objectives: 1\) To explore the atypical inter-brain synchrony underlying social impairment in children with ASD through the implementation of hyperscanning techniques. 2\) To elucidate the connections between inter-brain synchrony and other characteristics of ASD social impairments. 3\) To construct computational models that can forecast the longitudinal development of ASD based on a multi-modal interpersonal synchrony profile.
